Neurons

An artificial neural network consists of many interconnected units of computation that are modeled after the elecrically-activated cells in our brains and spinal cords. Neurons recieve inputs from other activated neurons, and when the weighted sum of those inputs exceeds a threshold, it becomes active itself.

Mathematically speaking, the input to a neuron is the linear function

$( f(x_0, x_1)=w_0 x_0 + w_1 x_1 )$

where

$( x_0, x_1 )$ are the outputs of the incoming neurons, and

$( w_0, w_1 )$ are how much each of those inputs is weighted.
